Petra

Petra is a historical and archaeological city in southern Jordan. It is famous for its rock-cut architecture and water conduit system. Petra was the capital of the Nabataean Kingdom and is believed to have been established as early as 312 BC. The city is renowned for its stunning facades carved into the rose-red sandstone cliffs, including the iconic Treasury (Al-Khazneh) and the Monastery (Ad-Deir). Petra was designated a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1985 and is considered one of the New Seven Wonders of the World. It attracts tourists from around the globe who come to explore its ancient ruins and marvel at its unique beauty.
